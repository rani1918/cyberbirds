Cyber Security Tips

  1. Protect yourself with these essential security practices

1. Use Strong and Unique Passwords

Create complex passwords with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters. Avoid personal information or common words. Use a password manager to securely store them.

2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Always enable 2FA on your accounts. It adds an extra layer of protection by requiring a second form of verification, such as a code from your phone.

3. Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your OS, browser, antivirus, and other software to patch security vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates when possible.

4. Be Cautious on Public Wi-Fi

Avoid sensitive activities on public networks. If necessary, use a VPN to encrypt your connection. Turn off file sharing when on public Wi-Fi.

5. Beware of Phishing Attacks

Don't click suspicious links or download attachments from unknown emails. Verify senders and look for spelling errors in messages.

6. Delete Unused Accounts

Unused accounts can become vulnerable over time. Regularly clean up and delete accounts you no longer use to minimize risks.

7. Monitor Account Activity

Regularly check your account activity and device logs. Enable notifications for suspicious logins or unknown devices.

8. Download from Trusted Sources

Only install apps from official stores. Avoid pirated software which may contain malware. Check app permissions before installing.

9. Cover Your Webcam

Use a physical cover or disable your webcam when not in use. Hackers can remotely access cameras on compromised devices.

10. Regular Data Backups

Maintain backups of important data on external drives or cloud storage. This protects against ransomware and accidental loss.
